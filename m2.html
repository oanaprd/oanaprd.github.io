How We Identified the Problem

  • The team shared firsthand experienced of wanting to live more sustainably but struggled to maintain consistent actions.
  • We reviewed existing sustainability apps and observed the gaps in their offerings. Users often complained about the lack of personalization or clear, actionable steps.
  • Examining competitors revealed that while many apps exist for specific actions, there was no integrated solution that connected users to a community and provided feedback, rewards, and an offset option in one place.

The Solution We Thought Would Address the Problem

  • Allow users to track their carbon footprint in real time through daily habits like transportation, diet, and energy use.
  • Based on the tracked activities, offer tailored advice for reducing their environmental impact.
  • Let users join challenges, share achievements, and interact with other users in local or global communities.
  • Users could offset their carbon footprint by investing in verified projects directly through the app.

Customer Discovery Plan Overview

  • Are users interested in tracking and reducing their carbon footprint?
  • Do they want a gamified, community-driven solution to stay engaged?
  • Are they willing to pay for a platform that integrates sustainability tracking, suggestions, and offsetting?

Customer Discovery Process in Detail

1. Identifying Our Customer Segments

  • Environmentally Conscious Individuals: Millennials, Gen Z, and families interested in sustainability.
  • Corporates & Small Businesses: Companies aiming to engage employees in sustainability initiatives.
  • Educational Institutions: Schools and universities integrating sustainability into their curriculum.

2. Reaching Our Customers

We used multiple methods to connect with potential customers:

  • Social Media: Engaging on platforms like Instagram, Twitter, and LinkedIn.
  • Eco-Events & Meetups: Attending green tech fairs and eco-friendly workshops.
  • Online Communities & Forums: Joining sustainability-focused discussions on Reddit and Facebook groups.

3. Collecting Information from Customers

We used the following tools to gather feedback:

  • Focus Groups: Virtual and in-person sessions with sustainability enthusiasts.
  • Face-to-Face Interviews: One-on-one interviews to understand their habits.
  • Online Questionnaires/Forms: Surveys embedded on our landing page.
  • Email Sign-ups: Building a list of interested users for further research.

Questions We Asked During Discovery

  • What motivates you to adopt sustainable habits (e.g., environmental concerns, cost-saving, convenience)?
  • What barriers do you face when trying to reduce your carbon footprint or live sustainably?
  • How do you currently track or monitor your sustainability efforts (if at all)?
  • Would you be interested in an app that helps you track your carbon footprint and offers rewards for eco-friendly actions?
  • How likely are you to join sustainability challenges with friends or within a community?
  • Would you be willing to pay for features that help you offset your carbon footprint or provide personalized recommendations?
  • What additional features would make such an app more appealing to you?

Key Findings

  • High Interest in Sustainability Tools: 85% of respondents wanted to track their environmental footprint.
  • Need for Motivation: Over 70% would be more motivated by rewards or community elements.
  • Personalization is Key: Respondents wanted tailored tips for their habits.
  • Offsetting as a Valuable Feature: 60% would purchase offsetting credits directly through an app.
  • Demand for Gamification: 65% preferred apps with eco-friendly challenges and gamified features.

How These Insights Have Affected Our Product

  • We integrated features like badges, challenges, and rewards to keep users motivated and enganged.
  • We enhanced the recommendation engine to deliver tailored actions based on the user's lifestyle.
  • We prioritized features that enable users to form or join sustainability-focused groups, encouraging collaboration and shared goals.
  • We focused on making the offset feature simple and integrated withing the app, making it easy for users to neutralize their carbon footprint directly.

Are We Ready for Customer Validation?

  • Based on our discovery process, there is a clear demand for a holistic sustainability platform. 85% of respondeds expressed interest in using such an app.
  • The feedback confirmed that users are motivated by gamification, community engagement, and personalization - features that are core to EcoPal.
  • A significant portion of respondents showed interest in premium features and carbon offset purchases.
  • Early-stage prototypes were well-received, with users suggesting only minor tweaks, which we are incorporating.
